# AI in Business — 15-Minute Talk

**Presenter**: Vishal Sachdev
**Audience**: 6+ Unit 4 CTE business teachers (high school)
**Slot**: ~15 minutes within the 8:15–9:00 am "Current and Emerging Trends" block
**Follows**: Kristina Wright & Amanda Branter on career and market insights

---

## Talk Structure (15 min)

### 1. The Headline (1 min)

> "Every business career your students are preparing for already involves AI — most people just don't notice it yet. Within 5 years, it won't be in the background. It'll be the primary way work gets done."

Frame: AI literacy is becoming as fundamental as spreadsheet literacy was 20 years ago.

### 2. Three Shifts Happening Right Now (6 min)

**Shift 1: From Data Entry to Data Direction** (~2 min)
- Old: Bookkeepers enter transactions. Marketing assistants compile reports. Admins schedule and organize.
- New: AI handles the rote work. The human decides *what questions to ask*, *what data matters*, and *what to do with the answer*.
- **CTE connection**: Students who can interpret AI output and make judgment calls are more valuable than students who can do the task manually.
- Quick example: Show an AI doing a competitive analysis in 30 seconds vs. hours of manual research — but point out what it gets wrong without human review.

**Shift 2: From Specialists to "T-Shaped" Builders** (~2 min)
- The most in-demand business roles now combine domain knowledge + AI fluency.
- Examples of emerging roles:
  - **AI-Augmented Marketing Analyst** — uses AI for customer segmentation, writes the strategy
  - **Business Process Automation Specialist** — maps workflows, directs AI to automate them
  - **AI Product Manager** — doesn't code, but shapes what AI products do
- **CTE connection**: Students don't need to learn to code. They need to learn to *describe what they want built* clearly — that's context engineering, and it's a business skill.

**Shift 3: From Consuming Content to Building Tools** (~2 min)
- The old model: learn software (Excel, QuickBooks, Canva).
- The new model: *build* the tool you need using AI. A student can describe a customer intake form, an inventory tracker, or a marketing dashboard — and AI builds it.
- Optional micro-demo (60 sec): Show a single prompt → working business tool (e.g., a simple invoice calculator or customer feedback form) created in Google AI Studio.
- **CTE connection**: This is project-based learning on steroids. Students aren't just using tools — they're creating them.

### 3. What CTE Teachers Can Do Right Now (3 min)

**The opportunity**: CTE business teachers are *perfectly positioned* for this shift because you already teach practical, applied skills. AI doesn't change that — it amplifies it.

Three things to try:

1. **Add AI to one existing project** — "Use AI to draft your business plan, then critique what it got wrong." Students learn both AI fluency AND business judgment. Example: In an entrepreneurship class, have students generate a market analysis with AI, then identify the 3 biggest gaps or errors.

2. **Teach students to be reviewers, not just users** — The skill is evaluating AI output, not just generating it. Have students grade AI work using a rubric. They learn more about the subject by finding AI's mistakes than by doing the assignment from scratch.

3. **Have students build something** — Even a simple tool (a quiz, a calculator, a marketing mockup) built with AI teaches more about the technology than any lecture. Example: "Build a customer feedback form for a local business" using AI — students learn requirements gathering, iteration, and quality control.

### 4. Where This Leads: The Gies AI Skill Framework (3 min)

Show the Gies "Skill Framework for Gies Business Learners" — three tiers:

**AI Literacy** (foundation)
- Explain fundamental AI concepts and technologies
- Recognize AI applications across business, society, and personal domains
- Understand ethical considerations in AI implementation
- Critically evaluate AI outputs and limitations

**AI Competency** (applied)
- Use AI tools for business applications and workflows
- Solve business problems using AI-powered resources
- Interpret AI-generated insights for business context
- Collaborate effectively with technical specialists on AI projects

**AI Expertise** (leadership)
- Develop and deploy AI solutions
- Lead AI implementation projects and initiatives
- Think strategically about AI's transformative potential in business models
- Innovate new AI applications or frameworks

**Key message to teachers**: "Your CTE students arrive at college with domain knowledge that most freshmen don't have. That's the foundation of AI Literacy. If you add AI fluency to what you're already teaching, your students show up ready for AI Competency — they're ahead of the game."

Position CTE teachers as essential to the pipeline, not playing catch-up.

### 5. Bridge + Close (2 min)

> "I've been talking about AI in *business careers*. Later at 10:15, Jamie, Martin, and Jason will show you AI in the *classroom* — how to actually integrate these tools into your teaching. And if you want to see what experiential learning looks like at scale, Charlie's BUS 301 session at 11:10 is a great example."

Close with: "We're building this framework so every Gies graduate has at least AI Competency. You can give your students a head start. The question is whether they'll be the ones directing AI or the ones replaced by someone who can."

## Alternative Approaches (kept for reference)

### Option B: "The 60-Second Demo" (High-Impact, Low-Lecture)

Spend most of the time on a live demonstration:
1. **(2 min)** Frame: "Let me show you what your students' future coworker looks like."
2. **(5 min)** Live build: Take a real CTE business scenario and build it live with AI. Narrate your thinking — show context engineering in action.
3. **(3 min)** Debrief: "That took 5 minutes. Your students can do this."

**Pros**: Memorable, visceral, proves the point.
**Cons**: Risk of technical failure. Requires prep and backup.

### Option C: "Before/After" (Storytelling Approach)

Tell two stories of the same business task — one without AI, one with.

**Pros**: Narrative is engaging for a breakfast crowd.
**Cons**: Less interactive, no hands-on element.
